XYZ Company HR Policy Document

1. Introduction

Welcome to XYZ Company! Our mission is to foster a collaborative, respectful, and productive work environment. This HR policy is designed to guide employees and management in maintaining a harmonious workplace. By working with us, you agree to abide by the policies outlined in this document.

1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this document is to:

Provide clear expectations for workplace behavior.

Outline benefits and leave entitlements.

Ensure compliance with Egyptian labor laws.

1.2 Scope

This policy applies to all full-time, part-time, and contract employees at XYZ Company.

2. Workplace Conduct

2.1 Code of Conduct

Employees are expected to:

Treat colleagues with respect and professionalism.

Abide by the laws of Egypt and the internal policies of XYZ Company.

Avoid conflicts of interest and report any ethical concerns.

2.2 Anti-Harassment Policy

XYZ Company maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward harassment. Any form of harassment, whether verbal, physical, or written, is strictly prohibited. Employees are encouraged to report incidents confidentially to the HR department.

2.3 Disciplinary Actions

Violations of company policies may result in disciplinary action, including verbal warnings, written warnings, suspension, or termination. The severity of the action will depend on the nature of the violation.

3. Compensation and Benefits

3.1 Salary and Wages

Employees are compensated in accordance with their roles, experience, and the local market standards.

Salaries are paid on the 25th of each month directly to the employeeâ€™s bank account.

3.2 Health Insurance

XYZ Company provides comprehensive health insurance for all employees.

Employees may extend their coverage to dependents at a discounted rate.

3.3 Performance Bonuses

Annual performance reviews determine eligibility for bonuses.

Bonuses are distributed at the discretion of management based on individual and company performance.

4. Leave Policies

4.1 Annual Leave

Employees are entitled to 21 days of paid annual leave per year, as per Egyptian labor law.

Leave requests must be submitted at least two weeks in advance and approved by the direct manager.

4.2 Sick Leave

Employees are entitled to paid sick leave upon submission of a valid medical certificate.

Sick leave is capped at 30 days per year.

4.3 Maternity and Paternity Leave

Female employees are entitled to 90 days of paid maternity leave.

Male employees are entitled to five days of paid paternity leave.

4.4 Public Holidays

Employees are entitled to all public holidays recognized by the Egyptian government. These include, but are not limited to:

Eid al-Fitr

Eid al-Adha

Revolution Day (July 23rd)
